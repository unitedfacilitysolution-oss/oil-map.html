Regional Intelligence

Key Producing and Refining Regions

Middle East The World's Largest Export Region

Saudi Aramco, ADNOC, KPC, INOC, and QatarEnergy collectively control the majority of global proven reserves. The Persian Gulf handles over 20 Mbpd of crude exports — approximately 40% of global seaborne crude trade.

West Africa Premium Light Sweet Crude

NNPC (Nigeria), Sonangol (Angola), and GNPC (Ghana) control the region's production. Bonny Light and Girassol grades are among the most sought-after globally. A primary UFS operational region.

Americas Fastest Growing Export Region

The United States, Brazil (Petrobras), and Guyana (ExxonMobil-led) are rapidly expanding export capacity. WTI and Brazilian pre-salt grades are in high demand from Asian and European refiners.

Asia Pacific World's Largest Import Region

SINOPEC, PetroChina, Indian Oil, and Japanese refiners collectively represent the world's largest crude import market. Over 60% of Middle East and African crude flows to this region.

Russia / CIS Sanctions-Affected Major Producer

Rosneft and Gazprom Neft control Russia's vast reserves. Urals crude rerouted from Europe to Asia since 2022. Kazakhstan's KazMunayGas exports Tengiz and Kashagan blend via CPC pipeline.

North Africa Mediterranean Gateway

NOC Libya, Sonatrach Algeria, and EGPC Egypt control significant reserves with direct pipeline and tanker access to European Mediterranean refineries. Light sweet crude grades command premium pricing.
